Community Stories

Shared experiences, shared strength

Every story matters. By sharing our experiences with mental health, we break down stigma and remind each other that we're not alone in these waters.

These stories come from real people in our community. Some share their names, others choose to remain anonymous—both choices are equally valid and respected here.

Stories of Hope & Resilience

Sarah M.

October 2024

"Three years ago, I couldn't get out of bed most days. Depression had taken over my life. Today, I'm not 'cured,' but I'm swimming. Some days are harder than others, but I've learned to ask for help, to be gentle with myself, and to celebrate the small victories. If you're struggling right now, please know: it can get better. You deserve support."

Depression Recovery

Anonymous

September 2024

"My anxiety made me feel like I was drowning. Social situations were terrifying. Through therapy and small daily steps, I've learned coping strategies that actually work. I still have anxious days, but now I have tools. To anyone feeling overwhelmed: you're not broken. You're learning to navigate difficult waters, and that takes courage."

Anxiety Therapy

Marcus J.

August 2024

"As a man, I was taught to 'be strong' and never show weakness. When I finally opened up about my struggles with PTSD, I learned that vulnerability IS strength. Asking for help wasn't giving up—it was fighting back. To all the guys out there suffering in silence: your mental health matters. It's okay to not be okay."

PTSD Men's Health

Anonymous

July 2024

"I lost my mom to suicide five years ago. The pain never completely goes away, but I've learned to carry it differently. I share my story because silence is what we need to break. If you're having dark thoughts, please reach out. Call 988. Text a friend. Your absence would leave a hole in this world that no one else can fill."

Grief Suicide Loss

Priya K.

June 2024

"Balancing school, work, and mental health felt impossible. Panic attacks before exams, constant worry about the future. I learned that self-care isn't selfish—it's necessary. Taking breaks, setting boundaries, and using campus counseling services changed everything. You don't have to have it all figured out. Just take it one day at a time."

Student Life Stress

Anonymous

May 2024

"Coming out was terrifying, and the rejection I faced triggered severe depression. But I also found an incredible community that accepted me exactly as I am. If you're LGBTQ+ and struggling, please know: you are valid, you are loved, and there are people who will embrace you. The Trevor Project saved my life, and I'm here today because I chose to stay."

LGBTQ+ Depression
