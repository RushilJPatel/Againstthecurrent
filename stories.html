Peer Stories

Real stories from real students about mental health, support, and hope

You're Not Alone

Sharing our stories helps break down stigma and shows others that they're not alone in their struggles. Every story matters, and every voice deserves to be heard.

Peer support means understanding each other's experiences. When we share how reaching out for help or connecting with friends made a difference, we create hope for others.

Share Your Story

All submissions can be anonymous. By sharing your story, you help others realize they're not alone and that peer support can make a difference.

Anonymous Recent

When a Friend Reached Out

"I was going through a really tough time during my junior year. I felt completely isolated and thought no one cared. Then one day, a friend I hadn't talked to in months sent me a simple text: 'Hey, are you okay? I've been thinking about you.'

That one message made me realize I wasn't invisible. We started talking regularly, and having someone who listened without judgment changed everything. Now I try to reach out to others, because I know how much it meant to me."

Peer Support Connection
Alex Recent

Breaking the Silence

"I used to think talking about mental health was weak. I struggled with anxiety for years, keeping it all inside because I was afraid of what people would think.

Then I saw a post from Against the Current about peer stories, and something clicked. I decided to tell a close friend. Instead of judging me, they said, 'Me too. I've been dealing with something similar.'

That conversation opened up a whole new level of friendship. We check in on each other now, and I realize that talking about it actually made me stronger, not weaker."

Anxiety Breaking Stigma
Anonymous Recent

The Check-In That Changed Everything

"I was in a dark place, thinking no one would understand. A classmate noticed I'd been quiet and asked if I wanted to talk. At first I said no, but they kept checking in—not pushy, just genuinely caring.

Eventually I opened up, and they listened. They didn't try to fix everything, they just made me feel heard. That connection helped me realize I could reach out for professional help too.

Sometimes the biggest thing someone can do is just be there and listen. That's what peer support means to me."

Depression Support
Jordan Recent

Finding My People

"I felt like I was the only one struggling with stress and feeling overwhelmed. School, college applications, family stuff—it all felt like too much.

I joined a peer support group at school (something I never thought I'd do), and it was life-changing. Just being in a room with other students who got it, who understood without me having to explain everything.

We created a group chat where we check in on each other. Having that support network made all the difference. Now I know I don't have to swim against the current alone."
