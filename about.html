About Against the Current

Our Mission, Vision, and Focus

Our Mission

Against the Current is dedicated to promoting mental wellness among high school students through peer support and connectedness. We believe that when students feel they belong and can openly discuss mental health, protective factors increase and risk factors decrease.

Like koi fish swimming against the current, we're working to break down barriers, reduce stigma, and create a supportive community where every student feels valued and heard.

Protective Factor

Peer Support & Connectedness

Definition: When students feel they belong to a community and can talk about mental health openly and without judgment.

How It Helps:
  • Reduces feelings of isolation and loneliness
  • Creates safe spaces for open dialogue
  • Builds resilience and coping skills
  • Encourages early help-seeking behavior
  • Promotes a sense of belonging and identity

Research shows: Students with strong peer connections are significantly less likely to experience mental health crises and more likely to seek help when needed.

Risk Factor

Social Isolation & Stigma Around Talking About Mental Health

Definition: When students feel disconnected from peers and fear judgment or negative reactions when discussing mental health challenges.

Why It's Dangerous:
  • Increases risk of depression and anxiety
  • Delays help-seeking behavior
  • Exacerbates existing mental health conditions
  • Creates cycles of silence and suffering
  • Reduces access to support networks

Research shows: Social isolation and mental health stigma are leading contributors to youth mental health crises and suicide risk.

Why This Matters

We chose to focus on peer support and connectedness as our protective factor, and social isolation and stigma as our risk factor because:

1

Peer Influence is Powerful

Teenagers are more likely to listen to and trust their peers than adults. Peer support can be the first line of defense in mental health.

2

Stigma Creates Barriers

Fear of judgment prevents students from seeking help, creating dangerous delays in getting support during mental health crises.

3

Connection Saves Lives

Simple acts of reaching out, listening, and showing care can make the difference between isolation and hope.

4

We Can Make Change

By creating platforms for peer stories, providing resources, and encouraging connection, we're building a movement.
